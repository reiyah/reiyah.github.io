When I was eleven years old, I found out about the existence of a summer math camp named Epsilon. I had always liked math in school, and despite not being exceptionally passionate about the subject at the time, decided to apply to the camp. With a stroke of luck, I ended up getting accepted. I was excited about the opportunity to attend a math camp (something which I had never even heard about before), but also nervous. What if my math abilities weren’t on par with the other kids there?

My experience at Epsilon was, in short, life changing. In just a short two weeks, I realized that math was much more than the formulas and memorization I had always associated it with. Math was beautiful. In my first year at Epsilon, I learned about hyperbolic geometry and set theory, among much more. Upon leaving, I was striving to know more.

Since that time, I have attended math camps every summer. For the past three summers, I have attended the Honors Summer Math Camp (HSMC) at Texas State University, which has also had a profound impact on me. HSMC taught me about number theory, analysis, abstract algebra, and proof-writing, and allowed me to participate in a research project led by Professor Wade Hindes. Along with a research partner, I worked on a soon-to-be-published research paper entitled “Irreducible Polynomials in Quadratic Semigroups.” The preview of this paper can be seen here: https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.03856.

An article detailing my feelings about and experiences at HSMC can be found here: https://www.davidsongifted.org/gifted-blog/a-students-perspective-honors-summer-math-camp-hsmc/. This coming summer, I will be a counselor at HSMC, something I’m looking forward to very much!
